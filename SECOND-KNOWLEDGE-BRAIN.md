## Core Concepts & Frameworks

### 1. Copywriting Frameworks

| Framework | Full Name | Best For | Structure | Emotional Register |
|-----------|-----------|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| AIDA | Attention–Interest–Desire–Action | General awareness content, ads, emails | Hook → explain value → create desire → CTA | Aspirational |
| PAS | Problem–Agitate–Solution | Pain-point-driven content, healthcare, supplements | State problem → intensify pain → present solution | Empathetic-urgent |
| FAB | Features–Advantages–Benefits | Product descriptions, e-commerce, B2B | What it does → why that matters → how it helps you | Rational-functional |
| StoryBrand (SB7) | Story Brand 7 Framework (Donald Miller) | Landing pages, brand narrative, long-form | Character → Problem → Guide → Plan → CTA → Avoid failure → Success | Trust-building narrative |
| PASTOR | Problem–Amplify–Story–Transformation–Offer–Response | Long-form content, email sequences, testimonial-heavy | Story arc with transformation at the center | Motivational |
| BAB | Before–After–Bridge | Social proof, case studies, transformation content | Current pain → desired future → how to get there | Hopeful-aspirational |
| ACCA | Awareness–Comprehension–Conviction–Action | Educational content, health literacy campaigns | Educate first, build conviction, then CTA | Informational-credible |
| QUEST | Qualify–Understand–Educate–Stimulate–Transition | Niche audience targeting, premium products | Pre-qualify reader → show understanding → educate → create desire → CTA | Selective-premium |
| HSO | Hook–Story–Offer | Social media, short-form ads, native content | Pattern interrupt → relatable story → offer reveal | Engaging-conversational |
| 4Ps | Promise–Picture–Proof–Push | Email marketing, direct response | Bold promise → vivid picture → social proof → strong CTA | Direct-confident |

### 2. Evidence-Based Claim Standards

- **Tier 1 (Preferred):** Systematic review or meta-analysis in peer-reviewed journal
- **Tier 2:** Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT) with statistically significant results
- **Tier 3:** Cohort or observational study with n > 500
- **Tier 4:** Expert consensus statement (major medical/industry body)
- **Tier 5:** Industry survey or white paper (credible org, n > 200)
- **Tier 6:** Anecdote / testimonial (requires disclosure under FTC guidelines)

All marketing claims for healthcare and cosmetics must be supported by Tier 1–4 evidence or use approved "structure/function" language.

### 3. Brand Voice Dimensions

| Dimension | Description | Scoring Signal |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Tone Consistency | Formal/casual/playful level matches brand guidelines | Language register variance |
| Vocabulary Set | Brand-specific terms and avoid-words applied | Presence of off-brand phrases |
| Sentence Rhythm | Short punchy vs. long flowing sentences per brand style | Mean sentence length |
| Emotional Register | Fear/aspiration/empathy/humor blend matches brand | Sentiment distribution |
| Differentiation | Copy sounds like this brand, not a generic brand | Unique brand promise present |

## Regulatory Framework Reference

### Healthcare Marketing (FDA — United States)

| Rule | Scope | Key Constraint |
|------|-------|---------------|
| 21 CFR Part 202 | Prescription drug advertising | Must include brief summary of risks; no false/misleading claims |
| FD&C Act Section 502 | Drug labeling (incl. OTC) | All claims must be truthful, non-misleading |
| FDA Structure/Function Claims | Dietary supplements | May not claim to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent disease |
| FDA Cosmetic vs. Drug distinction | Cosmetics that affect body function = drugs | Claims like "rebuilds collagen," "stimulates hair growth" may reclassify a cosmetic as a drug |
| 21 CFR Part 101.93 | Structure/function claim notification | Must notify FDA within 30 days of marketing with such a claim |

**Safe Language Patterns (Supplements/Wellness):**
- ✅ "supports healthy immune function"
- ✅ "helps maintain normal blood glucose levels"
- ✅ "promotes relaxation"
- ❌ "treats inflammation"
- ❌ "cures insomnia"
- ❌ "prevents heart disease"

### Cosmetics Marketing (EU — EC 1223/2009 + EFSA)

| Rule | Key Constraint |
|------|---------------|
| EC 1223/2009 Art. 20 | Claims must be substantiated, truthful, fair, and not misleading |
| EFSA Regulation EC 1924/2006 | Health/nutrition claims must be on the EU Register of Authorized Claims |
| COSMETICS EUROPE Guidelines | Voluntary but widely adopted: 6 common criteria |
| UK ASA / BCAP Code | Post-Brexit: cosmetic claims must be substantiated; medical claims require medical authorization |

**Common Violations in Cosmetics:**
- ❌ "regenerates skin cells" (implies drug-like mechanism)
- ❌ "clinically proven" without specifying the study
- ❌ "heals eczema" (therapeutic claim → drug territory)
- ✅ "visibly reduces the appearance of fine lines in 4 weeks (based on consumer study, n=52)"
- ✅ "skin feels firmer after 2 weeks of use"

### Fashion Marketing — Sustainability Claims (EU Green Claims Directive + FTC Green Guides)

| Rule | Key Constraint |
|------|---------------|
| EU Green Claims Directive (2023, pending adoption) | Sustainability claims must be verified by independent third party; vague claims prohibited |
| FTC Green Guides 16 CFR Part 260 | Unqualified environmental claims are deceptive without substantiation |
| UK CMA Green Claims Code | Must be accurate, clear, not misleading; comparative claims must be fair |

**Greenwashing Triggers:**
- ❌ "100% sustainable" without certification
- ❌ "eco-friendly materials" without specifying what and how
- ❌ "carbon neutral" without referencing a verified offset standard
- ✅ "made with 80% recycled cotton (GRS-certified)"
- ✅ "carbon neutral delivery — offset via Gold Standard projects"

### FTC Endorsement Guidelines (16 CFR Part 255)

- All material connections (paid partnerships, gifted products) must be clearly disclosed
- "#ad," "#sponsored," "#gifted" are accepted disclosure formats
- Testimonials must reflect typical results; if not, a disclaimer is required
- Social media influencers are equally bound by these rules

### ASEAN Regulatory Landscape

| Rule | Scope | Key Constraint |
|------|-------|---------------|
| ASEAN Cosmetic Directive (ACD) | Harmonized cosmetic regulation across 10 ASEAN member states | Requires product notification before marketing; claims must align with ASEAN Cosmetic Claim Guideline |
| ASEAN Health Supplement Framework | Dietary supplements | Traditional use claims permitted with documentation; novel ingredients require safety assessment |
| Country-specific food/drug agency rules | Thailand FDA, Philippines FDA, Vietnam MoH | Advertising permits required in some markets; language-specific labeling rules |

## Category: Healthcare Advertising Rules

### [2026-06-15] FDA — Prescription Drug Advertising: Brief Summary Requirements (21 CFR 202)
- **Authors:** U.S. Food and Drug Administration
- **DOI/URL:** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-21/chapter-I/subchapter-D/part-202
- **Relevance:** Compliance framework for any healthcare content referencing prescription products
- **Key Finding:** All prescription drug advertising must include a "brief summary" of risks including all listed side effects, contraindications, and warnings. "Fair balance" requires presenting benefits and risks with comparable prominence. DTC ads must be understandable by a 7th-grade reading level.

### [2026-06-15] FDA — Structure/Function Claims vs. Disease Claims: Decision Framework
- **Authors:** U.S. FDA, Center for Food Safety and Applied Nutrition
- **DOI/URL:** https://www.fda.gov/food/food-labeling-nutrition/structure-function-claims
- **Relevance:** The primary decision framework for supplement marketing compliance
- **Key Finding:** Structure/function claims describe the effect of a product on body structure or function (legal for supplements). Disease claims describe the effect on a specific disease (legal only for approved drugs). "Supports healthy joints" = structure/function (legal); "treats arthritis" = disease claim (illegal for supplements). FDA provides 16 criteria for distinguishing the two.

### [2026-06-15] FTC — Health Claims in Advertising: Substantiation Requirements
- **Authors:** Federal Trade Commission
- **DOI/URL:** https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/health-claims-advertising
- **Relevance:** Defines the evidence standard for all health-related advertising claims
- **Key Finding:** FTC requires "competent and reliable scientific evidence" for health claims — typically at least one well-designed RCT. Comparative claims require head-to-head clinical evidence. Testimonials that imply typical results must reflect actual typical results; exceptional results require "results not typical" disclaimers.

### [2026-06-15] EMA — EU Directive on Advertising of Medicinal Products (2001/83/EC Title VIII)
- **Authors:** European Medicines Agency
- **DOI/URL:** https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32001L0083
- **Relevance:** EU compliance framework for pharmaceutical/healthcare advertising
- **Key Finding:** EU prohibits direct-to-consumer advertising of prescription-only medicines.
